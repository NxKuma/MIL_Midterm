  • BLOG #1

    What makes one media literate?

    In my opinion, an individual is literate in media and information if they are capable of consuming and sharing non-harmful knowledge that they found on the internet. This means that an individual can identify their discovery whether it is factual or fake using legitimate resources that they can find in the media. In this process, they can share useful information that is verified and can be consumed by other individuals. Practices that I have been using to promote my media and information literacy are fact-checking, information gathering, and information sharing. Fact-checking occurs by going through different legitimate websites on the internet whether the information given is factual or not. Next, Information gathering is when you collect the necessary context of the given information to fully understand and comprehend what the information is about. Lastly, information sharing occurs when the information given is verified and fully understood by myself to share it with social media platforms and my peers.

  • BLOG #2

    Evolving Media

    In the present time, media is the best way to get information and opinions about the way the world works. As information is free in a democratic society, one is free to form an opinion about the information they are given. Media plays its role as the main forebearer of news and information to the common people. This comes in the form of all available media but focuses more on what the times are interested in.

    Given that our available media is one with the vulnerability to be manipulated by an anonymous user, a lot of false news and information can spread around and affect other people. A rumor about another person can hinder their ability to get a job or even a place to stay. Media plays a vital role in our life which we take for granted. It influences us in our ability to choose and enjoy the things that we interact with within our daily lives. A simple review score can make a movie worth watching and this also applies to us people. What we see in the media gives us subtle hints on what we should like and can like. Even though we have the freedom to access the information that we have, we still have to conform to what the majority prefers.

  • BLOG #3

    Internet Addiction

    Internet addiction has been widely spread in the more recent generations now more than ever. This is caused by innovations that allow us to have the power of the internet in a portable device we call “the smartphone”. With smartphones being the main source of information, communication, and entertainment, people, especially teenagers, have their smartphones with them all the time. Teenagers use the internet quite differently from their peers and can get addicted to them for different reasons. The daily mail has found that girls use the internet more than boys do. They said that the main reason for this is that girls use their time on the internet to talk to their peers which can take up more hours than boys using the internet to play games. This is supported by an article by Claire Groden(2015) stating that “78% of girls who made friends online did so through social media, like Facebook or Instagram, while only 52% of boys made their online friends through social media. Instead, 57% of boys said they made their friends through online gaming, compared to 13% of girls.”

    This internet addiction isn’t exclusive to western countries where the mentioned researchers came from. One case of internet addiction is with my friend which we will call “Allen” for his anonymity. Allen has been brought up in a normal lifestyle up until he moved with his family here in Makati. Before the pandemic, Allen used to blow his allowance just to play computer games throughout the week. This wouldn’t be a problem at first since he still has good grades and homemade lunch to eat. He only got worse a year later when he lost 10kg because he would be playing instead of eating. We tried talking to him about it and it seemed to get better but it didn’t help. He only got better when we introduced him to swimming. He slowly got a bit healthier and learned to manage his time when playing computer games. This anecdote personifies two of the advice from the Tablet for Schools which are, “Know and keep your boundaries on the usage of the internet” and “Have some time doing things that don’t involve the use of smartphones or a digital device.”

  • BLOG #4

    #iNFLUENCE: The Power of Media and Information

    Media and information play a vital role in our Filipino society. Even before the concept of digital media was invented, Filipinos have a way of sharing information through word of mouth. Since the birth of social media, Filipinos have been using this platform to share what they see around their lives. Sharing information like that is very important for us to keep us up with the times that are constantly changing. With every new trend, news, gossip, or information, Filipinos share it through social media. This may seem like a good thing, but a lot of vulnerabilities can come from this. Trolling, crime, invasion of privacy, spreading of misinformation, etc. are rampant in these sorts of sites. Without proper knowledge of what can be trusted or not, a lot of Filipinos have exploited this and have been exploited by this. Information and media have the power to shape the beliefs and understanding of what we have in our current time. Power like this should be held responsibly by Filipinos who know better. We should be responsible for what we share and believe on those social media sites. Understanding what is fact from fake gives us the power to give other people the knowledge to do so as well. Correcting false information online in a reasonable manner keeps other people secure from believing and being tricked. As difficult as it is to convince other people to not be tricked by this false information, we are still responsible to teach them how to handle these types of information. Media has a gigantic and growing library of information that is either falsified or factual, teaching them how to disseminate between the two can and will help them.

  • BLOG #7

    #TEXT: What's in the message

    The tone of the first text feels a bit heart-warming and caring, It conveys a message of a promise that the person will always find the recipient no matter what happens. The second text conveys the feeling of horror and anxiety. It communicates to the viewer that they will find them in any way possible and make their life a living nightmare. If I received the first note from a friend, I would feel warm inside because it feels like they care about me and even if we part ways, we will still meet. In comparison, if I got the second note from a friend, I would run give toward safety because it feels like they are stalking me and every time I hide from them they would always find me.

    I believe that text-based messages are powerful because as seen in the two notes, the way the message is written conveys different feelings toward the viewer. Since there is no voice that you can hear in text-based messages, we try to interpret the message by the spacing between the letters or kerning, the font that is used, and even how it is capitalized.

  • BLOG #6

    #REiMAGINE: Branding Redefined

    This Redesign of the Famous Fast Food Chain tries to change the famous golden arches by splitting them away from one another to show their stance on social distancing during the pandemic. Along with the controversy that the redesign received on its release, it doesn’t really convey its message that well. A lot of this comes down to the unreadability of the logo, which confuses consumers such as myself when I see it. If something needs to change in this redesign, then it should be the tagline and not the logo. The simplistic logo of the golden arches doesn’t really go well with modifications unless its another variation of an M, but the tagline is a more flexible and easier way to convey a message whilst staying on brand.
